Why Slow Steiner?

  • There are four affordable housing buildings with hundreds of eldery and disabled residents within two blocks of Duboce Park. Many have expressed fear over fast-moving cars and unsafe street crossings. Our neighbor Michael was recently hit in his wheelchair by a car speeding down Steiner and turning up Duboce Ave. A safer and slower Steiner would provide neighbors of all ages and abilites a safer connection to the park.
  • As a part of The Wiggle, Steiner between Duboce and Waller is a pedestrian and bike artery across the city. A Slow Steiner would better support these already common uses of Steiner by reducing motor vehicle traffic and speed, providing a safer corridor for pedestrians and bicylists.

What is the background and context behind Slow Steiner?

Slow Steiner has grown out of DTNA's Vision 2030 initiative (née Slow Triangle). Grounded in the results of a series of community workshops and surveys starting in 2022 (1, 2), efforts have been focused on improving pedestrian safety and traffic calming throughout Duboce Triangle. Over the past year, based on community input, particular attention has been given to Sanchez and Steiner, and in particular the intersection at Duboce/Sanchez/Steiner. Wiggle Fest

In October 2023, we hosted our first Wiggle Fest, closing Steiner to cars between Duboce and Hermann. In addition to bringing together neighbors and building community, it was an opportunity to reflect and reimagine how Steiner could best serve the neighborhood.

Stay tuned for a second Wiggle Fest in Spring 2024!
